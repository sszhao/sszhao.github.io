Award-Winning Research
at ISS 2024

#ISS2024 #BestPaper #GestureGPT #HCI
Award Best Paper

I'm excited to share that our research paper, "GestureGPT: Toward Zero-Shot Free-Form Hand Gesture Understanding with Large Language Model Agents," has been honored with the Best Paper Award at the ISS 2024 conference.

This achievement is a testament to the hard work and dedication of my colleagues: Xin Zeng, Xiaoyu Wang, Tengxiang Zhang, Chun Yu, and Yiqiang Chen.

Research Impact

GestureGPT explores the intersection of spatial computing and generative AI, enabling systems to understand user intent through complex, non-predefined hand gestures without prior training.

Download Paper PDF

Our work at ISS 2024 emphasizes the shift towards more natural, fluid human-computer interaction (HCI) models. By leveraging Large Language Model agents, we bridge the gap between technical gesture recognition and human-centric design, allowing for "zero-shot" understanding that adapts to the user's natural movement rather than forcing the user to learn specific patterns.
